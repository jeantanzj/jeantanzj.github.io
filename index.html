Helping seniors to learn continuously
Winner of raiSE LeapForGood 2018
Keeping up with technology can be difficult. In Singapore, many seniors attend workshops to learn to use their smartphones for cashless payments, online shopping, and the like. A common complaint from seniors is that they forget what they've learnt after the class, and have no one to turn to for help. At Xiohoo, we not only provide the workshops, but also follow-up practice clinics, and eventually, a peer to peer support network.
Classroom Interaction Tool
Cerebral Palsy Alliance Singapore - Engineering Good
Uneven verbal ability amongst students in a class at CPAS makes it difficult for teachers to assess the understanding of the taught content. To provide a level playing field during quiz-time, my team conceived of hacked bluetooth mice connected to the students' choice of a trigger mechanism (different tactile mechanisms activated by hands, by their head etc). Their answers are sent to the teacher's laptop and collated.

Digitizing forms across Singapore's government
Any public officer with a email address from a whitelisted agency can create forms easily, as if they were using Google Forms or Typeform. The standardization of how forms look across government lends credibility and reduces phishing risk. Integrated with with Singpass and MyInfo, FormSG speeds up form-filling and provides accurate information to the form creator.
After joining the project in February 2019, one of the main features I led was the storage of responses that have been encrypted end-to-end. Launched in May 2019, this feature was critical for supporting applications for tickets to Singapore's National Day Parade. If you'll like to read more, my team-mates published an article behind the engineering. We were also featured in Singapore's national newspaper, reproduced below.
Improving the credibility of links shared by public officers
Go.gov.sg was conceived at Open Government Products' annual hackathon. We built it in response to the proliferation of shortened urls that were being shared by public officers to the citizens. bitly, tinyurl, and other services out there could be used by malicious actors pretending to be from the government, but Go.gov.sg limits access to public officers with .gov.sg email addresses, enabling them to create vanity urls without any tedious onboarding process.
Our engineering team of two built the React application from scratch, and deployed it on AWS. Together with FormSG, Go.gov.sg was used for citizens' applications for tickets to the national day parade in 2019 (https://go.gov.sg/ndp19).
Delivering soft copy medical certificates
Digital MC has been piloted at the National Heart Centre Singapore's SAF clinic, where servicemen no longer receive hard copy medical certificates unless specifically requested. Instead, they receive an SMS containing a link to their MC and can forward it easily to their supervisor.
In the development of this product, I made extensive use of the GovTech stack to communicate between the government intranet and internet.
Enabling speedier police investigations
Over the course of six months, I led the development of JARVIS to search through several of Singapore Police Force's databases, helping to improve the police's efficiency. The application has been well-received, and was highlighted on tech.gov.sg, our parent agency's website.
